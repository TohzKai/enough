# Enough — Competitor Landscape & Differentiation Verdict

_Companion to enough-moat-and-differentiation.md. Compiled 2026-07-01. Built from a 3-lens
web-research review (Singapore market / global decumulation software / adjacent & emerging).
Prices and product claims are 2025–26 vendor/marketing values — re-verify before the deck
asserts them. "UNVERIFIED" = inferred, not confirmed from primary source._

---

## 0. The verdict in five sentences

Enough is **not differentiated on the engine or the number** — the Monte-Carlo / guardrails /
withdrawal-sequencing stack is **mature and commoditised** globally (Boldin US$144/yr,
ProjectionLab US$129/yr, Income Lab for advisers), and the whole *concept* — "treat the state
annuity as a floor and tell retirees how much they can safely spend" — is **already productised
at national scale in Australia** (Otivo, Aware Super's "income confidence score"), whose
super + means-tested Age Pension is the closest analogue to CPF + CPF LIFE. So the idea is a
**known overseas pattern re-pointed at CPF, not a novel invention.** BUT within Singapore *today*
there is a **genuine, currently-unoccupied niche**: no SG product combines neutrality + whole-
wealth aggregation + a confidence-based "safe-to-spend" number + native CPF-LIFE/SRS depth + a
family layer. The honest conclusion: **Enough is differentiated *enough to launch into a real
gap*, but not enough to be *safe* — the gap is a positioning/packaging gap, not a technology
moat, and two SG players each hold half of it and could close it with one feature ship.**

---

## 1. The two findings that reframe everything

**(a) The engine is table stakes, not a differentiator.** Globally, a probabilistic safe-to-spend
engine is a checkbox at consumer prices. Monte Carlo (1,000–2,000 paths → probability-of-success),
tax-aware withdrawal sequencing across account types, and even risk-based dynamic guardrails
(Fitzpatrick/Tharp, productised by Income Lab) are all shipping. **ProjectionLab already lists a
Singapore tax preset** — the generic international engine nominally reaches SG already. _Implication:
if Enough leads the deck with "8 models / Monte Carlo," a sharp grader who can Google Boldin and
Income Lab will see commodity tech. Lead with neutrality + CPF-native depth + family, not the engine._

**(b) The concept is proven — and being rolled out to millions — in Australia.** Australia's
Retirement Income Covenant legally obliges super funds to help members decumulate, so "digital tool
that tells you how much you can safely spend and how long your money lasts, integrating the
means-tested Age Pension floor" is becoming standard national infrastructure: **Otivo** (neutral-ish
licensed digital advice, whole-wealth + Age Pension, free to all Australians to 30 Jun 2026 then
paid, also B2B2C via funds), **Aware Super "Retirement Manager"** ("how much can I spend / how long
will it last" + an "income confidence score" — essentially Enough's value prop, live), and
**AustralianSuper × Ignition** for 3.6m members. _Implication: validated demand and proven UX to
learn from — but also proof a well-funded Otivo-like or a super fund could pivot into SG._

## 4. The differentiation that actually survives the scan

Only three things survive — and **none of them is the engine**:

1. **Neutrality married to whole-wealth aggregation.** Banks aggregate (DBS/OCBC via SGFinDex) but
   are *not neutral*; MoneyOwl is neutral but *not yet decumulation*; robos are *neither* (AUM
   funnels). The intersection — a neutral app that aggregates everything and tells you how much to
   spend — is unoccupied **today**.
2. **Native CPF-LIFE / SRS / SG-tax depth, delivered neutrally and direct-to-consumer.** Global
   tools have the engine but not native CPF logic (CPF LIFE's three plan types + deferral bonus as a
   guaranteed annuitised floor; SRS 10-year / 50%-taxable withdrawal sequencing; OA/SA/RA/Medisave
   interactions). Providend has the depth but only via a human adviser. This is real, but it is an
   **erodible localisation lead**, not a structural moat — a funded incumbent could rebuild it.
3. **The family / adult-child layer.** Appeared in **none** of the competitors surveyed — SG or
   global. This is the single cleanest, most uncontested whitespace. _(Note: this is the product
   feature — roles/permissions/co-sign — not the parked question of making adult children the primary
   buyer. The scan says the family layer is worth keeping as a differentiator regardless of the GTM
   decision.)_

## 5. The threats that make Enough *assailable* (why "enough to launch" ≠ "safe")

- **DBS NAV Planner — the capability twin.** Already aggregates whole wealth + projects retirement
  cashflow + has CPF/SRS data + millions of pre-authenticated users. Adding a confidence-based
  safe-spend number is incremental. Its only structural weakness is **neutrality** (it exists to
  sell DBS products) — which is precisely Enough's wedge against it.
- **MoneyOwl (Temasek Trust) — the positioning twin.** Free, neutral, SG, trusted, distribution at
  scale; one decumulation module from Enough's exact spot. You **cannot out-credibility a Temasek
  entity on neutrality** — you have to out-*focus* it (be the dedicated decumulation product before
  it ships a module) or partner.
- **Providend — the philosophy twin.** Owns the exact thesis (CPF-LIFE-floor, decumulation-first,
  neutral) with deep credibility; a direct threat *if it productises*, and a natural **partner or
  acquirer** if it doesn't.
- **An Otivo-like / super-fund pivot from Australia.** The playbook is built and funded there; SG
  entry is a localisation project, not an invention.
- **The engine commoditisation + ProjectionLab's SG preset** — the "international engine" is no
  longer scarce.

## 6. Is Enough sufficiently differentiated? — the honest answer

**Differentiated enough to enter a real, currently-open Singapore niche — NOT enough to be
defensible on the product alone.** The differentiation is in **positioning + SG-native depth +
neutrality + the family layer**, not in technology, and Enough sits squeezed between DBS (has the
capability, lacks neutrality) and MoneyOwl (has the neutrality + trust, lacks the dedicated
decumulation engine) — either of whom could close the gap with a single feature ship. That means
**defensibility cannot come from the idea; it must be built** — exactly the wedge-to-moat plan in
`enough-moat-and-differentiation.md` (neutrality bound to the licence-gated system-of-record +
family embedding + being first to a credentialed, downturn-survived track record + speed).

Put plainly for the deck: **Enough is a focused fast-follower of a proven overseas pattern into a
temporarily-open SG gap.** That is a legitimate, fundable position — *if* the team is honest about
it and competes on focus, SG-native depth, neutrality, the family layer, and execution speed, rather
than claiming a novel engine or an inherent moat. Over-claiming novelty is the fastest way to lose a
sharp grader who can find Boldin, Income Lab, Otivo, and MoneyOwl in five minutes.

## 7. What to do about it (recommendations)

1. **Re-pitch away from the engine.** Demote "8 models / Monte Carlo" to hygiene; lead with
   *"the only neutral, whole-wealth, CPF-native safe-to-spend plan — for the whole family."*
2. **Double down on the 3 survivors**, especially the **family layer** (the only uncontested
   whitespace) and **CPF/SRS native depth** (what global tools and ProjectionLab's generic SG preset
   can't match).
3. **Win the two races, don't fight the two giants head-on:** out-*focus* MoneyOwl (be the dedicated
   decumulation product first); out-*neutral* DBS (a bank structurally can't). Treat **Providend** and
   **insurers** as potential channel/partners, not just rivals.
4. **Learn the UX from Australia** — "income confidence score" (Aware Super) is a proven pattern;
   Otivo's free→paid + B2B2C-via-funds is a tested monetisation/distribution model.
5. **Resolve the two existential unknowns before betting** (below) — they gate the differentiator and
   the business model respectively.

## 8. Verify before the deck asserts (load-bearing unknowns)

1. **SGFinDex eligibility for a non-FI.** SGFinDex has ~15 *licensed-FI* participants; there is no
   public "any app can connect" tier. Whether Enough can connect without being (or partnering with) a
   licensed FI is **UNVERIFIED** and **gates the whole-wealth-aggregation differentiator** — confirm
   with MAS/SNDGG.
2. **Willingness to pay for neutral planning.** Both neutral SG planning ventures struggled:
   **MoneyOwl's** original NTUC version wound down (Aug 2023) explicitly because clients valued
   planning but **wouldn't pay planning fees**; **MyMoneySense** (free, government) exited Dec 2025.
   Neutral-planning monetisation is the venture's central *commercial* risk — reinforces the
   "monetise the family / B2B2C via aligned partners" model, not retiree B2C subscriptions.
3. **DBS NAV Planner's actual decumulation depth** (deterministic gap vs probabilistic safe-spend) —
   determines how big DBS's remaining gap really is.
4. **Providend** — any signal of a digital/self-serve RetireWell product (would collapse the
   philosophy-twin gap).
5. **CPF LIFE / FRS / ERS current figures** (carried from the risk doc) — for the SG-depth claims.
